# GarageBuild Manifesto

> *AI is temporary. Software is permanent.*

*Build Once. Run Anywhere. Use Any AI. Own Everything.*

---

## Where This Began

Over the past year, I spent time with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, LM Studio, Ollama, Continue, VS Code, Cursor, and a dozen enterprise AI platforms.

Almost every session eventually led back to the same question:

> "Why can't these tools work together while letting me stay in control?"

That question became GarageBuild.

Not because AI tools are bad. Many of them are extraordinary.

But because they are fragmented, cloud-dependent, and built around today's models rather than tomorrow's software.

---

## Software Development Has Changed

For decades, developers wrote software.

Today, developers collaborate with AI.

Tomorrow, AI will be a standard part of every software team.

Yet today's AI development landscape is fragmented.

One tool writes code. Another previews applications. Another runs local models. Another deploys. Another manages prompts. Another remembers context. Another locks you into its cloud.

**Instead of one development environment, developers stitch together a dozen disconnected products.**

The problem isn't the tools. The problem is the assumption underneath them — that AI development must happen in a proprietary cloud, tied to a specific model, controlled by someone else's infrastructure.

That assumption is wrong.

---

## We Started Asking A Different Question

Instead of asking: *"Which AI model is best?"*

We asked: *"Why isn't there an open platform where any AI model can help me build software?"*

That question became GarageBuild.

Not another chatbot. Not another IDE. Not another cloud platform.

An open, local-first, model-agnostic development environment that runs wherever you need it and belongs entirely to you.

---

## What We Believe

### AI Is Temporary. Software Is Permanent.

Today's best model will not be tomorrow's best model. Technology changes. Pricing changes. APIs change. Providers come and go.

But the software you build may live for years. It will outlast every model that helped create it.

**GarageBuild is never built around today's AI. It is built around software.**

That single belief explains every architectural decision we've made — the plugin system, the model abstraction layer, the local-first design, the portable project format. None of it is accidental. All of it follows from this.

### AI Providers Are Plugins — Not The Platform

Today you might use GPT-4o. Tomorrow you might use Claude. Next year something we haven't heard of yet.

Your workflow shouldn't change. Your code shouldn't change. Your infrastructure shouldn't change.

In GarageBuild, AI providers are plugins. They can be swapped, combined, upgraded or replaced without touching a single line of your project.

**The model serves the developer. Not the other way around.**

### Developers Should Own Their Work

Your ideas belong to you. Your source code belongs to you. Your infrastructure belongs to you. Your data belongs to you. Your AI provider should be your choice.

Software should not be held hostage by subscriptions, proprietary runtimes or vendor lock-in.

**Everything GarageBuild generates is standard source code. No proprietary format. No lock-in. No strings attached.**

### AI Should Run Where You Need It

Sometimes that's your laptop. Sometimes it's your company datacenter. Sometimes it's a private cloud. Sometimes it's a public cloud. Sometimes it's an air-gapped server with no internet connection.

**The software adapts. The developer doesn't.**

### Software Development Is A Craft

Software is not merely generated. It is designed. Refined. Reviewed. Tested. Improved.

AI should amplify that craft — not replace it.

**The developer remains the architect. AI becomes the collaborator.**

### Platforms Should Be Open

Great platforms outlive the technologies they run on.

Git survived every IDE that came and went. Docker survived every container runtime that competed with it. Linux survived every commercial operating system that tried to replace it.

They survived because they were open, extensible, and independent of any single vendor.

**GarageBuild isn't being built around today's AI models. It's being built around openness.**

## Why I Built This

I don't want another AI chatbot.  
I don't want another code editor.  
I don't want another cloud platform.

I don't want to spend my time copying prompts between applications. I don't want my workflow tied to whichever model is popular this month. I don't want to choose between privacy and productivity. I don't want to lose ownership of my code because a platform generated it.

**I want one workspace. One platform. My models. My infrastructure. My workflow. My code.**

I've spent years building enterprise AI platforms. I know that the most valuable systems are never the ones tied to a single model. They are the ones that abstract complexity, preserve flexibility, and remain useful as technology evolves.

That's why GarageBuild doesn't exist to compete with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Cursor, or Lovable.

**GarageBuild exists because software deserves an open foundation — independent of whichever AI model happens to lead the benchmark this year.**

---

## The Philosophy In One Sentence

**Open. Local. Extensible. Private. Developer-first.**

Every architectural decision in GarageBuild follows from this.

- Plugin-first architecture — because extensibility is non-negotiable.
- Local-first design — because privacy is non-negotiable.
- Model agnostic — because freedom is non-negotiable.
- Code ownership — because trust is non-negotiable.
- Open source — because transparency is non-negotiable.

---

## Our Goal

To become the operating system for AI-assisted software development.

Not by owning your code. Not by owning your models. Not by owning your infrastructure.

**But by giving you complete freedom to choose all three.**

We measure success simply:
- Developers enjoy using the platform.
- Projects remain portable.
- Organizations can self-host.
- New AI providers can be added without modifying the core.
- Developers always know exactly what they've spent and what they own.
